Criteria

Part II paragraph 39(2c) of F.A.R 2004 L.I 1802 requires that the head of accounts section of a department shall control the disbursement of funds and ensure that transactions are properly authenticated to show that amounts are due and payable. Also, Standard practices requires that payments made to individuals / staff shall be signed by each individual to show that monies paid were dully received by them.

Condition

On the contrary, the Accounts Unit failed to ensure that individuals / staff sign for monies claimed to have been paid to them. See Appendix ‘H’ for details.

Cause

These lapses could be ascribed to laxity on the part of the accounts department to ensure that beneficiaries signed for monies received.

Effect

The authenticity of such transaction could not be ascertained and could also lead to staff not receiving monies due them.

Recommendation

We recommend that the Accountant should ensure that the payees append their signatures to authenticate the payments or such monies refunded.
