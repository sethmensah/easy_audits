3.0.0 Scope of Audit

Pursuant to the objectives mentioned below, we reviewed the internal controls over the generation, accounting and reporting of revenue and expenses for its adequacy and effectiveness for the period under review.

The exercise was expected to cover the following areas;

  1. Analysis of IGF usage
  2. Review of working capital
  3. Review of procurement activities and
  4. And other areas that may need attention.

We further interviewed people for additional information which we considered necessary for the purpose of the audit.

4.0.0 Audit Objectives

It was in pursuant of the objectives below that the records of the {{data.facility_name | title}} were audited for the period under review.

4.0.1 IGF Usage

With regards to expenditure, the objective was;

  1. To ensure that payments were made for goods and services received only.
  2. To ascertain the control and management of cash and evaluated them against the relevant provisions of Financial Administration Regulation, Accounting, Treasury and Financial Reporting rules and instructions.
  3. That any expenditure from the IGF is valid and adequately supported by all relevant documents of expenditure.
  4. That relevant laws and regulations, policies and procedures are followed in incurring expenditures
4.0.2 Working capital
  1. To ascertain whether {{data.facility_name | title}}, upon valuing its stocks/assets in addition to accounts receivable and bank balances, would be able to pay off all indebtedness when due.
4.0.3 Procurement
  1. To ascertain management adherence to procurement procedures as stipulated in the Public Procurement Act, 2003 (Act 663).
5.0.0 Audit Methodology

In compliance with the above objectives, the general control environment of the facility was assessed and checked for their level of compliance with;

  1. Provisions of the Financial Administration Regulation (FAR).
  2. Public Procurement Act, 2003 (Act 663)
  3. Ghana Health Service (GHS) policies and procedures enshrined in the ATF rules and instructions in relation to revenue collections and bank lodgments
5.0.1 Pre-audit Activities

To ensure that our audit met the expectations of stakeholder, we designed audit program (check list) to guide the audit team to perform specific audit task gathering, analyzing and evaluating evidence. We organized a short period training to equip the team to accomplish the intended objectives necessary, to obtain sufficient competent evidential matters responsive to the audit objectives.

5.0.2 On-Site Audit Activities

We began the on-site audit with an entrance meeting with auditee management team. At this meeting we discussed key issues relating to the audit for cooperation and support of management for timely completion of the assignment. This was followed with examination of records and documents as well as interviews, where necessary, to obtain sufficient competent evidential matters responsive to the audit objectives.

5.0.3 Post Audit Activities

Exit conference was held with management members of {{data.facility_name | title}}. The primary purpose was to ensure that together we arrive at mutual understanding of the findings, and propose recommendations to be included in the final Audit Report.
